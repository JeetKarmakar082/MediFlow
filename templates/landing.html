How It Works

From arrival to discharge

A patient's journey through MediFlow in 6 steps.

Step 1

Patient Registration

Staff registers the patient digitally — name, age, blood group, allergies, and emergency contact. Existing patients are found instantly by phone or name search.

Step 2

Vitals & Triage Assessment

A nurse enters BP, pulse, SpO₂, temperature, pain scale, and symptom description. Gemini AI returns a triage score 1–5 with clinical reasoning in under 3 seconds.

Step 3

Token Issued

A queue token is issued for the relevant department. Score 1 & 2 patients are auto-routed to Emergency with a token already created — no manual steps needed.

Step 4

Priority Queue Ordering

The queue orders by triage score first, then arrival time. A critical patient arriving at 3pm is seen before a minor case from 9am — automatically, every time.

Step 5 (if needed)

Emergency Escalation

If a waiting patient deteriorates, staff raises an emergency alert. The system bumps their token to priority 1 and logs the escalation with full traceability.

Step 6

Consultation & Medical Record

Doctor marks the token as done. A medical record is created with diagnosis, prescription, and follow-up date — permanently linked to the patient's history.

Data Architecture

9 models. Fully relational.

Every entity is a proper Django model with foreign keys, indexes, and admin integration.

Patient

Demographics, blood group, allergies, emergency contact. The root entity everything links to.

TriageAssessment

Vitals, symptoms, AI score, reasoning, action, and source (Gemini or rule-based).

Token

Queue entry linking patient to department with triage priority, status, and timestamps.

EmergencyEscalation

Emergency alerts linked to triage assessment and queue token with resolution tracking.

Doctor

Profile, specialization, department, registration number, and live availability flag.

DoctorSchedule

Weekly schedule per doctor — day, start/end time, max patients, active flag.

Department

Named departments with unique codes — scopes queues, doctor assignments, and filters.

MedicalRecord

Visit history — diagnosis, prescription, notes, follow-up date, linked to doctor.

Notification

SMS notification log — patient, message, type, status, and Twilio response tracking.
